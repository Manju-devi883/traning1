# 1. Physical Identity & Access Management (PIAM)

## Theory
Physical Identity and Access Management (PIAM) is a security system used to manage and control who can enter physical locations such as offices, data centers, laboratories, and other restricted areas.

PIAM ensures that only authorized individuals can access specific locations based on their roles and permissions. It combines identity management with physical security systems to improve safety and compliance.

### Why PIAM is Important?
- Prevents unauthorized access
- Improves workplace security
- Manages employee and visitor access
- Tracks entry and exit records
- Supports security audits and compliance

---

# Key Components of PIAM

### Identity Management
Stores and manages information about employees, contractors, and visitors.

### Authentication
Verifies a person's identity before granting access.

Examples:
- ID Card
- RFID Card
- Fingerprint
- Face Recognition
- PIN Code

### Authorization
Determines which areas a person is allowed to access based on their role.

### Access Control
Allows or denies entry to secured locations using access control devices.

### Audit Logs
Maintains records of every access attempt, including date, time, and location.

# Example

An employee scans their RFID access card at the office entrance.

The PIAM system verifies the employee's identity and checks whether they have permission to enter that area.

If authorized, the door unlocks automatically, and the system records the entry time in the access log.
